InEye support for early access users

InEye is an AI playground for supported Meta wearables with camera streaming, AI assistance, WhatsApp-based display messaging, social streaming, photo capture, and lower-distraction video recording.

View Privacy Policy Troubleshooting

Core features

  • Live video streaming from supported wearables
  • Real-time AI assistance with visual input
  • Photo capture and local video recording
  • Social streaming to supported RTMP platforms

Display answers

InEye can generate answers and route them through WhatsApp so they can appear on supported display-enabled devices when that workflow is configured.

Important note

Feature availability depends on hardware support, Meta app configuration, permissions, WhatsApp availability, network quality, and third-party service setup.

Quick start checklist

  1. Install the Meta companion app on the iPhone paired to your wearable.
  2. Enable Developer Mode on the supported device when required.
  3. Open InEye and complete device registration.
  4. Grant Bluetooth, microphone, photo, and related permissions as prompted.
  5. Configure AI, messaging, or streaming integrations only for the features you plan to use.

Troubleshooting

The wearable does not connect

Confirm the device is powered on, paired in the Meta companion app, within range, and allowed to use Developer Mode if your workflow requires it. If registration was interrupted, complete it again from InEye.

Live video stays on waiting or never starts

Check that the device is connected, camera access has been granted through the Meta companion app, and the app has completed registration. Closing the wearable or losing Bluetooth connection can stop the stream.

AI answers do not appear in the display

That flow depends on WhatsApp-based delivery and supported display-enabled hardware. Verify the WhatsApp integration is configured correctly and that the target device is able to show the resulting messages.

Social streaming does not go live

Verify your RTMP destination, stream key, and platform limits. InEye currently supports workflows such as Instagram Live, Facebook Live, YouTube Live, and Twitch through supported RTMP targets.

Recording works, but the output is not saved to Photos

InEye can record video locally inside the app. Saving to the photo library requires photo access on iOS. Open Settings for the app and enable photo library access if needed.

Why use InEye recording instead of the default recording flow?

InEye is designed to support a more focused recording workflow with less visual interruption for supported use cases, which can be more comfortable during movement, creation, and hands-free capture.

Compatibility

  • Social streaming depends on valid RTMP credentials
